— FREQUENTLY (NERVOUSLY) ASKED

Compliance & conscience.

Most questions can be answered by reading our 47-page Master Services Agreement. Below is a curated selection.

Is this legal?

+
In most jurisdictions, yes. In several others, only after a brief regulatory adjustment period. Shameify ships with a built-in "Legal Mode" that automatically dilutes language and removes named individuals based on your office's IP geolocation. Our compliance posture is best-in-class among similarly-positioned platforms (n=1).

Doesn't this lower morale?

+
Yes. Morale is a lagging indicator. Output is a leading indicator. We are very focused on output.

Can employees opt out?

+
Technically. Opting out is itself an infraction (50 points) and is publicly logged. Several customers have reported a 0% opt-out rate after their first quarter.

How does the AI know what counts as an infraction?

+
Our model is fine-tuned on 14 years of HR investigations, post-mortem documents, and the collected Slack DMs of middle managers (with consent, mostly). It operates with a precision-recall trade-off skewed toward recall. We err on the side of documentation.

What integrations do you support?

+
Slack, Microsoft Teams, Google Workspace, Outlook, Zoom (transcripts), Notion, Linear, Asana, Jira, Greenhouse, Workday, Lattice, 15Five, Bonusly, Confluence, GitHub PR titles, calendar metadata, and any system that emits webhooks. We also accept paper memos.

What data does Shameify collect?

+
All of it. We then discard most of it for storage reasons. The rest is retained indefinitely and surfaced contextually.

Can I delete my shame history?

+
Per GDPR Article 17, yes, with 30 days' notice. The deletion is itself recorded as an event titled "Attempted Erasure of the Record" (75 points). Your new history begins immediately upon deletion of the previous one. The cycle is the feature.

Is this a real product?

+
Define "real."
